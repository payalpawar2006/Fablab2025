LASER CUTTING

Laser cutting is a modern manufacturing process that uses a high-powered laser beam to cut different types of materials. The laser focuses intense light energy into a small point, generating enough heat to melt, burn, or vaporize the material along the cutting path. The process is computer-controlled, which makes it highly accurate and reliable for creating both simple and complex shapes. It can be used on a wide variety of materials such as wood, acrylic, plastic, cardboard, fabric, and metals.
Since the tool does not physically touch the surface, there is less chance of damage or distortion to the material. Laser cutting is commonly guided by CAD (Computer-Aided Design) files that provide exact shapes and designs to be cut with precision. Different types of lasers are used depending on the material, such as COâ‚‚ lasers for non-metals and fiber lasers for metals. The process is not only useful for cutting but also for engraving designs on surfaces. It provides smooth edges, reduces material waste, and requires little finishing work. Laser cutting is faster than many traditional cutting methods and is widely used in industries like automotive, electronics, signage, jewelry, and manufacturing.
The thickness of material that can be cut depends on the laser power, and more powerful lasers can cut through thicker metals. Safety precautions are very important because the laser beam can cause burns or eye damage, and exhaust systems are often used to remove harmful fumes and particles. This technology has become essential for creating prototypes, models, machine parts, and decorative items. Laser cutting combines speed, precision, and flexibility, making it one of the most efficient methods of modern fabrication.
